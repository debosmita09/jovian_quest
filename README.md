# Jovian Quest

## Jovian Quest: A Journey Through Jupiter and Its Moons

### A: Project Description

I plan to create a simple browser-based game called ‘Jovian Quest’, where players can explore Jupiter and its four largest moons, namely: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto. As players interact with the game environment, they will learn more about each celestial object’s size, mass, atmosphere (if present), and other cool scientific features. The game will include short quizzes and challenges so that players can test their knowledge while progressing through missions. 

The game will also demonstrate the significance of the study of these celestial objects for further comprehension of the history of formation and evolution of our Solar System. This is important as the strong gravitational field of Jupiter had a great impact on the formation of the Solar System. In addition, the moons of Jupiter like Europa and Ganymede may be containing subsurface oceans and therefore are some of the most prospective objects for the search of the habitats suitable for life. Thus, I will try to bring together the entertainment and science to present an entertaining but informative way to know more about Jupiter!


### B: Questions to be Answered

1.	What are the major physical characteristics of Jupiter and its four Galilean moons?
2.	How do the surfaces and environments of Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto differ from one another?
3.	Why are Jupiter and its moons important for understanding the formation of our Solar System and the search for potential life supporting environments?


### C: Project Presentation

I will present my project as an educational game made with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. It will consist of an interactive map of Jupiter and its four major moons. The game will begin with a brief in orbit around Jupiter when the player selects which moon to land on. Each moon destination will contain an interactive world with animations that will show players the science behind the moon's surface, atmosphere, interior and interesting facts from past and current space missions about them.

Throughout the investigation of each location, players will get assigned various missions including identification of geological formations on the moon, matching each moon to its characteristics, analysis of data and completing short quizzes on the information gained from exploring the destination. In doing so, they will earn points which will allow them to proceed further in the game, unlocking new destinations while making each challenge increasingly difficult. There will also be a mission log where all the scientific facts that the players learn will be recorded and players can review them easily before moving on to the next location.
I will be walking through the game during the presentation and showing clips of the game being played. My goal is to encourage users to actively explore and contemplate on the Jovian system while reinforcing key concepts learnt in class in a fun and memorable way.
